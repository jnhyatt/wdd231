What happens after launch?

Does launching satellites leave space junk in orbit forever? When a satellite reaches the end of its life, its orbit is slowed until it falls back to earth, safely burning up in the atmosphere. Satellites in higher orbits are placed in graveyard orbits.

How do rockets work?

Newton's third law says that for something to move forward, it has to push something else backward. In the vacuum of space, there's no ground or air to push against, so rockets have to carry their own reaction mass. Carrying an entire rocket to orbit would require a massive amount of fuel, so rockets are designed with multiple stages, which can be jettisoned when they are out of fuel to reduce weight.

Satellite

An object in orbit around a planet or other celestial body. Artificial satellites are used for various purposes, including communication, weather monitoring, and scientific research.

Graveyard Orbit

A high, stable orbit used to safely store retired satellites, keeping them out of busy operational zones like geostationary orbit.

Stage

A section of a rocket with its own engines and fuel. Most rockets use multiple stages, discarding each one when it runs out of fuel to reduce weight.
